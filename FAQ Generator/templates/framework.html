Frequently Asked Questions

 

What parts do I need for my p320 conversion?

If you already have a Sig P320 fire control kit (FCU), caou can easily convert it into a different size or style P320 using a conversion kit. You could also build a completely custom P320 by mixing and matching your favorite pars, such as slides, barrels, grip modules, sights and magazine release.

What parts do I need to complete a Sig p320 fire control unit(FCU)? 

Completing a Sig Sauer P320 Fire Control Unit (FCU) typically involves assembling the core components of the FCU. To fully complete your P320 FCU, you will need the following essential parts:

  1. Trigger Assembly: This includes the trigger bar, trigger bar spring, and trigger shoe. These components are crucial for the firing mechanism and trigger pull.
  2. Slide Catch Lever and Spring: The slide catch lever holds the slide open after the last round is fired. It includes the lever itself and the accompanying spring.
  3. Takedown Lever and Spring: The takedown lever is responsible for disassembling the firearm. It also includes the associated spring.
  4. Trigger Bar Spring: This spring helps control the trigger bar's movement.
  5. Grip Module: The grip module houses the FCU and provides the frame for your P320. It's the part you hold and interact with when shooting.
  6. Magazine Catch: This component is essential for releasing and securing magazines.
  7. FCU Chassis: The FCU chassis is the central component that houses the trigger assembly, sear, and other critical parts. It's the heart of your P320.
  8. FCU Pins: These pins secure various components within the FCU chassis and ensure proper alignment.
  9. Sear Housing Assembly: The sear housing assembly includes components like the sear and sear spring, which play a crucial role in trigger function and safety.
  10. Safety Lever and Spring: If your P320 has a manual safety, these parts are necessary for its operation.

Please note that the exact parts and their configurations may vary slightly depending on your specific P320 model, generation, and any optional features. Additionally, be sure to follow all firearm safety guidelines and local laws when assembling or modifying your firearm. If you're unsure about the assembly process, consider seeking guidance from a qualified gunsmith or referring to Sig Sauer's official documentation.
